# REIT-Valuation-and-Financial-Model.

    Introduction: 
The REIT Valuation & Financial Model is a comprehensive real estate investment model that analyses, value and assess the investment attractiveness of REITs. The model applies valuation techniques that determine the intrinsic value of the REITs. Unlike traditional operating companies whose value is largely driven by earnings growth, REITs derive their value from income generating real estate assets, predictable cash flows and long-term appreciation in property values. As a result, valuation focuses not only on accounting profits but also on the underlying value of the real estate portfolio, recurring operating income, dividend sustainability and future cash flow generation.
This model combines financial statement analysis with multiple valuation including Net Asset Value (NAV), Dividend Discount Model (DDM), Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) and Capitalisation Rate Analysis to provide a comprehensive assessment of shareholder value. Rather than relying on a single valuation technique, the model triangulates value across several approaches, enabling investors to compare outcomes and make well informed investment decisions.

    Understanding REITs:
REITs is an investment vehicle that pools capital from multiple investors to acquire, develop, manage or finance income producing real estate assets. Instead of purchasing an entire commercial property, investors purchase units or shares in the REIT, allowing them to participate in large scale real estate investments while benefiting from professional management, diversification and liquidity.
The primary objective of a REIT is to generate sustainable returns for investors through rental income, capital appreciation or value created through property development. Depending on its investment strategy, a REIT may focus on generating stable recurring income from completed properties or creating value through the development and eventual sale or transfer of new real estate projects.
This model analyses the two principal REIT structures commonly encountered in the market: the Income REIT (I-REIT) and the Development REIT (D-REIT).

    Income REIT (I-REIT): 
What is an I-REIT: An Income REIT invests in completed, operational and income-generating properties such as office buildings,mixed use development etc.  
The primary objective of an I-REIT is to generate stable and predictable rental income while preserving and enhancing the long-term value of its property portfolio. Since the underlying assets are already operational, investors receive relatively consistent cash distributions supported by recurring rental income.

## How an I-REIT Creates Value

Investment cycle: Property Acquisition → Property Management → Rental Income Collection → Operating Expense Management → Net Operating Income (NOI) → Dividend Distribution → Capital Appreciation.

# How Investors Earn Returns

Investors in an Income REIT generally receive returns from two complementary sources:

1. Dividend Income: Rental income generated by the property portfolio is distributed to shareholders as periodic dividends, providing a stable stream of cash income, Higher dividend yield indicates stronger cash return.

   Dividend Yield % = ( Market price per share/ Annual dividend per share) 
	​
2. Capital Appreciation: Over time, well managed properties may increase in market value due to rental growth, improved occupancy, asset enhancements and favourable market conditions. As the value of the underlying portfolio increases, the NAV per share may also increase, resulting in capital gains for investors.

To compute: 

a)  Share Price Capital Appreciation % = (( Purchase share price(Original purchase price paid by the investor) / (Current share purchase price(Currentmarket price of the unit))

b)  NAV Growth % = ((Current NAV per share- Previous NAV per share)/ Previous NAV per share))

    Development REIT (D-REIT): 

It focuses on creating value by developing new real estate projects rather than acquiring completed income producing properties. Investors commit capital during the development phase with the expectation that value will be created through planning, construction, leasing, stabilisation and eventual sale, refinancing or transfer of the completed asset.

Unlike an Income REIT, where returns are primarily generated from recurring rental income, a Development REIT creates shareholder value through the successful execution of development projects and the increase in asset value from inception to completion.

The lifecycle of a Development REIT can be effectively understood using the PRINCE2 method, where each stage represents a key milestone in transforming an initial investment into a completed real estate asset.
